3D reconstruction was one of the holy grails of 3D Computer Vision in the hope to make 3D representations more readily available without intense amounts of labor to generate 3D models. Differentiable rendering and optimization has made immense progress in that direction. At the same time, with similar motifs, image and video generative models have already completely changed how we think about the way we create these types of media. What are the lessons we need to learn from both of these endeavours and can these two domains converge?

Andrea Vedaldi
Professor, University of Oxford | Meta

3D generative AI as a foundation of spatial intelligence

‚ñ∂ Abstract

The development of advanced spatial intelligence is likely to be one of the most important goals of computer vision in the years to come. Supported by advances in generative Artificial Intelligence (AI), language models have evolved into extraordinarily powerful tools for abstract reasoning, but their grasp of physical reality is tenuous. This is vividly demonstrated by the fact that even the best image and video generators often produce non-physical hallucinations, despite incorporating language models. In this talk, I will suggest that 3D computer vision, especially when combined with generative AI, will be a key to developing future AIs that are spatially aware. I will illustrate this point by showcasing some of the work done by my teams at the University of Oxford and Meta. First, I will introduce the first neural network that can outperform traditional techniques from bundle adjustment, achieving robust, general, and efficient feed-forward 3D reconstruction. This is a significant leap that opens a path to develop 3D-aware foundations of computer vision. I will also briefly touch on a new representation for dynamic 3D content that may be the basis to further extend such models to capture motion. I will then consider generative models as a way to capture rich prior information about 3D objects, including their internal structure and composition. Finally, I will demonstrate the power of off-the-shelf 3D generators in the creation of complex 3D content, including immersive 3D worlds.
